# Handoff: Skyrim Drag & Drop Mod

**Date:** 2026-04-22  
**Version:** v0.1.23-alpha  
**Status:** Working — grab, drag, drop, and throw all functional

---

## What Works

- **Grab**: LesserPower spell with GrabActor archetype, self-contained (no Seize NPCs dependency)
- **Drag**: Havok mouse spring handles physics, NPC follows player via spring
- **Drop (G key / R tap)**: Destroys springs, dispels GrabActor on both player + NPC, delayed velocity zero on all ragdoll bodies next frame
- **Throw (R hold)**: Charges over configurable time, zeros all ragdoll bodies next frame then applies impulse to every body in camera forward direction
- **INI Config**: All tunable values read from `SKSE/Plugins/DragAndDrop.ini`
- **Target filtering**: Dead/paralyzed always valid, followers/children/anyone configurable via INI
- **ESP conditions**: Tautology (`GetDead==1 OR GetDead==0`) passes all actors, C++ does real filtering

## What Doesn't Work Yet

- **Force ragdoll on stiff NPCs**: ForceRagdoll() exists but causes slow-mo/weapon offsets when called at grab time
- **Stamina drain**: Stub exists, not wired to frame tick
- **Throw damage/stagger**: Not implemented
- **Knockout mod reload fix**: Stashed in Skyrim_KnockoutPatched, caused issues

## Key Architecture Decisions

1. **Drop mechanic**: Delayed task (`SKSE::GetTaskInterface()->AddTask()`) zeros velocity on ALL ragdoll bodies next frame — must wait for engine to process spring release
2. **Throw mechanic**: Same delayed-zero approach, but applies impulse to ALL bodies (not just spring body) after zeroing — single body gets dampened by ragdoll constraints
3. **Impulse scale**: Removed `BS_TO_HK_SCALE` (0.014) from impulse calc — it was making impulses 70x too weak. Raw direction × force × mass
4. **Spring energy**: The Havok spring stores kinetic energy in all 20 ragdoll bodies during drag. Without zeroing, NPCs fling on release regardless of impulse
